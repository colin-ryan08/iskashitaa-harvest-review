Initial onboarding requirements

What has to be true before anyone touches a tree, and how a new volunteer moves from orientation into the progression path described in Roles & Expectations.

Before Day One

Entry requirements

There is exactly one prerequisite for a first harvest. No prior experience, equipment ownership, or scheduling history is required — only orientation and a signed waiver.

Step 1 prerequisites: Completed volunteer orientation and signed waiver. No harvest experience required.

Every new volunteer enters the program at Step 1 — Hand Picker, regardless of background. From there, advancement runs on demonstrated technique, not tenure or a fixed schedule — see Step 1 in Roles & Expectations for the full responsibilities and advancement criteria.

What to Bring

Gear requirements

The same baseline gear applies at every step of the progression path — nothing additional is required to advance.

Closed-toed shoes

Required for all on-site roles.

Long sleeves

Sun and scratch protection while picking.

Hat & sunscreen

Arizona harvest conditions, most of the year.

Reusable water bottle

No on-site water service.

How Advancement Works

How the progression path works

All new volunteers begin at Step 1 after completing orientation and signing the volunteer waiver. They advance sequentially as the Harvest Lead assesses their readiness — typically after 2–3 harvests demonstrating careful, consistent technique at the current step. Returning or experienced volunteers may be placed at an appropriate step directly by the Harvest Lead rather than restarting at Step 1.

Sequential by default

Steps 1–9 build skill incrementally; each one is a prerequisite the Harvest Lead signs off on before the next.

Two flexible steps

Steps 10 (Branch Raker) and 11 (Homeowner Interface) may be performed by experienced volunteers regardless of strict sequential completion.

Two separate tracks

Harvest Lead and Driver do not require completion of the full progression — they're distinct roles with their own prerequisites. See Roles & Expectations.

Open Items

What onboarding still needs decided

The progression path above describes how advancement is meant to work. Four mechanical questions are still open from the June 2026 program review, each with a designated decision-maker.

  • Tracking mechanism — how will each volunteer's current step actually be recorded? (leadership / volunteer coordinator)
  • Advancement criteria — should the "2–3 harvests" guidance become a fixed number, or stay a Harvest Lead judgment call, per step? (harvest leads / leadership)
  • Ownership — who enforces the path day-to-day, across harvests and Harvest Leads? (leadership)
  • Waiver linkage — confirm the orientation/waiver process is formally tied to Step 1 entry, not just assumed. (leadership)

The Salesforce data model proposed in Salesforce Evaluation & Proposal is built to answer the tracking-mechanism question directly — a volunteer's progression step becomes a field on their Contact record, updated by the Harvest Lead after each harvest.
