Initial onboarding requirements

What has to be true before anyone touches a tree, and how a new volunteer moves from orientation into the tiered role model described in Roles & Expectations.

Before Day One

Entry requirements

There is exactly one prerequisite for a first harvest. No prior experience, equipment ownership, or scheduling history is required — only orientation and a signed waiver.

Hand Picker prerequisites: Completed volunteer orientation and signed waiver. No harvest experience required.

Every new volunteer enters the program as a Hand Picker in the Foundational tier, regardless of background. From there, moving into other roles or tiers runs on demonstrated comfort, not tenure or a fixed schedule — see Hand Picker in Roles & Expectations for the full responsibilities and readiness criteria.

What to Bring

Gear requirements

The same baseline gear applies across every tier and role — nothing additional is required to take on a new role.

Closed-toed shoes

Required for all on-site roles.

Long sleeves

Sun and scratch protection while picking.

Hat & sunscreen

Arizona harvest conditions, most of the year.

Reusable water bottle

No on-site water service.

How It Works

How the tiered role model works

All new volunteers begin with Hand Picker after completing orientation and signing the volunteer waiver. From there, roles are organized into three tiers — Foundational, Experienced, and Specialist — rather than a single fixed sequence. Within each tier, roles are listed in a suggested order, but volunteers may try them in any sequence the Harvest Lead feels is appropriate. Moving between tiers is based on demonstrated comfort and readiness, not a fixed harvest count. Returning or experienced volunteers may be placed at an appropriate tier directly by the Harvest Lead.

Three tiers, suggested order

Foundational, Experienced, and Specialist. Order within a tier is a suggestion, not a requirement — the Harvest Lead sequences roles to a volunteer's interest and fit.

Staying is valid

Remaining within a tier long-term is a complete, supported contribution. Experienced and Specialist roles still require demonstrated experience, and a few — like Ladder Picker after Ladder Spotter — carry a non-negotiable prerequisite.

Two separate tracks

Harvest Lead and Driver do not require completion of any tier — they're distinct roles with their own prerequisites. See Roles & Expectations.

Open Items

What onboarding still needs decided

The tiered role model above describes how movement between roles and tiers is meant to work. Four mechanical questions are still open from the June 2026 program review, each with a designated decision-maker.

  • Tracking mechanism — how will each volunteer's current tier and the roles they're cleared for actually be recorded? (leadership / volunteer coordinator)
  • Readiness criteria — should the "2–3 harvests" guidance become a fixed number, or stay a Harvest Lead judgment call? (harvest leads / leadership)
  • Ownership — who maintains the model day-to-day, across harvests and Harvest Leads? (leadership)
  • Waiver linkage — confirm the orientation/waiver process is formally tied to Hand Picker entry, not just assumed. (leadership)

The Salesforce data model proposed in Salesforce Evaluation & Proposal is built to answer the tracking-mechanism question directly — a volunteer's tier and roles become fields on their Contact record, updated by the Harvest Lead after each harvest.
