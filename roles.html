04 · Who

Roles & expectations

Every role in the program, organized into three tiers — Foundational, Experienced, and Specialist — plus two supporting roles. Prerequisites, responsibilities, the skills each role needs, and how a volunteer can move between tiers when ready. All new volunteers begin with Hand Picker after orientation. Staying within a tier long-term is a complete, valued contribution.

How It Works

The tiered role model

All new volunteers begin with Hand Picker after completing orientation and signing the volunteer waiver. From there, roles are organized into three tiers — Foundational, Experienced, and Specialist — rather than a single fixed sequence. Within each tier, the roles below are listed in a suggested order, but that order is not mandatory: volunteers may try roles in any sequence the Harvest Lead feels is appropriate. Moving between tiers is based on demonstrated comfort and readiness, not a fixed harvest count.

Staying within a tier long-term is a valid, supported outcome. Not every volunteer needs to move into Experienced or Specialist roles. That said, Experienced and Specialist roles still require demonstrated experience before a volunteer takes them on, and a few carry a specific, non-negotiable prerequisite — such as Ladder Picker requiring prior Ladder Spotter experience. Harvest Lead and Driver remain separate tracks that do not require completion of any tier.

Foundational Comfortable, low-risk entry roles. Every volunteer starts here — no harvest experience required. Staying here long-term is a complete, valued path

Includes Hand Picker, Ground Gatherer, Crate Transporter, and Branch Raker — suggested order shown below, but volunteers may try them in any sequence the Harvest Lead feels is appropriate.

1

Hand Picker

Foundational · Suggested order 1 of 4 — Entry Point

Pick low-hanging fruit by hand from the tree. Entry point for all new volunteers — no direct supervision required after orientation.

Prerequisites: Completed the required onboarding session, signed waiver, and coordinator approval to harvest. No prior harvest experience required.

Responsibilities
  • Pick fruit by hand from lower branches of trees or bushes — fruit reachable without a tool or ladder.
  • Learn to identify harvest-ready fruit vs. unripe or overripe fruit.
  • Use correct grip and gentle twist-and-pull to remove fruit without damaging the branch.
  • Place (do not drop) fruit gently into collection buckets or crates.
  • Follow any site-specific instructions given in the pre-harvest briefing.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • No prior harvesting experience required.
  • Completed orientation and signed waiver.
  • Willingness to observe and follow instruction.
  • Appropriate gear: closed-toed shoes, long sleeves, hat, sunscreen, water bottle.
Where it can lead: After 2–3 harvests demonstrating careful, consistent picking technique, volunteers may try other Foundational roles, move into Experienced roles such as Picker with Tool, or continue hand-picking long-term — all are valid, at the Harvest Lead's assessment.

This is the mandatory starting point for every new volunteer, regardless of which tier they may move into later — no one skips Hand Picker. No direct supervision is required once orientation is complete, but the Harvest Lead is always present on-site.

2

Ground Gatherer

Foundational · Suggested order 2 of 4

Collect fruit placed on or fallen to the ground. Keep the harvest zone clear and safe.

Prerequisites: Comfortable with Foundational-level picking.

Responsibilities
  • Walk the harvest zone, collecting fruit placed by pickers or fallen from the tree.
  • Distinguish between fruit worth collecting and fruit too damaged to use.
  • Place collected fruit gently into crates — do not overfill (prevents bruising).
  • Keep the ground clear and safe for the rest of the team.
  • Call out any safety hazards: bees, uneven ground, falling debris.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable with Foundational-level picking.
  • Basic fruit quality awareness from picking roles.
  • Ability to bend, crouch, and move around the harvest area comfortably.
Where it can lead: Volunteers showing good fruit-quality judgment and reliable habits may be ready to try Ladder Spotter (Experienced, safety-critical) when interested — or continue with Foundational roles indefinitely; both are valid.
3

Crate Transporter

Foundational · Suggested order 3 of 4

Move loaded crates from the harvest site to the vehicle without damaging the produce.

Prerequisites: Comfortable with Foundational-level roles.

Responsibilities
  • Receive filled crates from pickers and gatherers and carry them to the vehicle.
  • Keep crates level with both hands — do not tilt, swing, or stack carelessly.
  • Do not overfill crates; follow Harvest Lead guidance on fill level.
  • Stack crates in the vehicle per the Harvest Lead's instructions (heavier produce on the bottom).
  • Briefly inspect each crate during transport — flag obviously damaged or molding fruit to the Culler.
  • Return empty crates to the picking area promptly.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable with Foundational-level roles.
  • Physical ability to carry 15–30 lbs across uneven terrain.
  • Awareness that bruising during transport accelerates spoilage in storage.
Where it can lead: Once the Harvest Lead confirms reliable, damage-free transport, volunteers may explore Weigher (Experienced) or other Foundational roles — or continue transporting crates long-term.
4

Branch Raker

Foundational · Suggested order 4 of 4

Leave every site better than you found it.

Prerequisites: Comfortable in Foundational roles (full picking and transport experience recommended).

Responsibilities
  • Rake or collect fallen branches, leaves, and debris from the harvest area after picking is complete.
  • Bag waste appropriately — separate compostable material if the homeowner has facilities.
  • Do a final walk-through of the site before the team departs.
  • Return all Iskashitaa tools (rakes, tarps, ladders) to the vehicle.
  • Confirm with the Harvest Lead that the site is clean and ready for the homeowner close.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable in Foundational roles.
  • Thoroughness and pride in site stewardship.
  • Physical ability to rake and bag debris.
Where it can lead: Often performed alongside other roles by experienced volunteers. Volunteers ready for more responsibility may explore Experienced or Specialist roles — or continue as Branch Raker long-term; both are valid.
↓ Moving into Experienced roles. Volunteers comfortable and consistent in Foundational roles may try Experienced roles when ready. There's no fixed harvest count or mandatory waiting period — readiness is assessed by the Harvest Lead, and remaining in Foundational roles long-term remains entirely valid.
Experienced Requires demonstrated Foundational comfort plus a specific skill or focus. Equally valid to remain here long-term

Includes Picker with Tool, Ladder Spotter, Weigher, and Recorder — suggested order shown below, assigned by the Harvest Lead based on a volunteer's interest and fit.

1

Picker with Picking Tool

Experienced · Suggested order 1 of 4

Extend your reach. Use a specialized fruit-picking tool to harvest mid-canopy fruit without a ladder.

Prerequisites: Comfortable, consistent hand-picking technique from Foundational roles (2–3 harvests recommended).

Responsibilities
  • Use Iskashitaa's fruit-picking tool (a basket or claw on an extendable pole) to harvest fruit from mid-canopy branches.
  • Learn correct tool handling: extension, positioning, and controlled fruit release into the basket.
  • Transfer fruit from the tool basket to collection crates gently to avoid bruising.
  • Keep the tool under control at all times — do not swing or extend near other volunteers.
  • Return the tool to its storage position when not in use.
  • Alert the Harvest Lead if the tool is damaged or malfunctioning.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable, consistent hand-picking technique (2–3 harvests recommended).
  • Comfort with extended-reach tool use; upper-body control to position the pole with precision.
  • Spatial awareness to operate safely around others.
Where it can lead: Often paired with Ground Gatherer during the same harvest, at Harvest Lead discretion. Volunteers comfortable here may explore other Experienced roles or remain focused on tool-assisted picking long-term.

Receive a brief demonstration from the Harvest Lead before first use of the picking tool. The tool significantly extends harvesting range and reduces the need for ladders on shorter trees.

2

Ladder Spotter Safety Critical

Experienced · Suggested order 2 of 4

Stabilize the ladder and watch the climber. This is a safety-critical role — no multitasking permitted.

Prerequisites: Comfortable in Foundational roles. Must be 16 or older.

Responsibilities
  • Position at the base of the ladder and hold it firmly with both hands throughout the climb.
  • Keep eyes on the climber at all times — do not look away or engage in side conversation.
  • Alert the Harvest Lead immediately if the ladder shifts, the climber shows difficulty, or any concern arises.
  • Keep the area around the base of the ladder clear of other volunteers and obstacles.
  • Do not perform any secondary task while spotting.
  • Follow all ladder safety instructions provided by the Harvest Lead.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable in Foundational roles.
  • Calm, focused temperament — safety is the only priority in this role.
  • Sufficient physical strength to hold a ladder steady against lateral movement.
  • Ability to communicate clearly and quickly in an emergency.
Where it can lead: Reliable spotting across 1–2 harvests, confirmed by the Harvest Lead, is required before moving to Ladder Picker (Specialist) — this dependency is not skippable. Many volunteers remain in Ladder Spotter long-term, which is equally valid.

Spotters must not multitask — this is non-negotiable. Volunteers under 16 may not serve as ladder spotters.

3

Weigher

Experienced · Suggested order 3 of 4

Operate the scale. Weight data feeds Iskashitaa's impact reports and homeowner communications.

Prerequisites: Comfortable in Foundational roles.

Responsibilities
  • Set up the scale at the harvest site and verify it is zeroed before use.
  • Weigh each crate of harvested produce and call out the reading clearly to the Recorder.
  • Protect the scale from the elements during use.
  • Communicate cumulative totals to the Recorder at close of harvest.
  • Return the scale to its case and to the vehicle at close of harvest.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable in Foundational roles.
  • Numeracy — comfortable reading a scale accurately.
  • If a reading seems off, weigh twice and flag the discrepancy to the Recorder.
Where it can lead: Once consistent accuracy is demonstrated, volunteers may pair this with Recorder — on small harvests these are often the same person — or continue as Weigher long-term; both are valid.

Weight data by address is entered into QuickBooks and used for homeowner impact reports and grant reporting. On small harvests, Weigher and Recorder may be the same person.

4

Recorder

Experienced · Suggested order 4 of 4

Keep the harvest record. Data logged here is the foundation of all impact reporting.

Prerequisites: Comfortable in Foundational roles.

Responsibilities
  • Record all harvest data on the harvest sheet or digital form: site address, fruit type(s), weights (from Weigher), date, volunteers present.
  • Keep recording materials organized and protected from the elements.
  • Communicate completed records to the Harvest Lead at close of harvest.
  • Hand all records to the Harvest Lead for return to the office for QuickBooks entry.
  • Flag any discrepancies or missing data to the Harvest Lead before departure.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable in Foundational roles.
  • Organized and detail-oriented; legible handwriting or comfort with digital entry.
  • Familiar with the harvest data fields and their downstream purpose.
Where it can lead: Volunteers with consistently accurate, complete data may be ready for Culler — In Situ (Specialist, quality-critical) once they've reviewed the written culling criteria. Remaining as Recorder long-term is equally valid.

Records feed QuickBooks entry, Salesforce homeowner outreach, and annual impact reports — incomplete records have real downstream consequences. Recorder and Weigher may be the same person on small harvests.

↓ Moving into Specialist roles. Specialist roles require demonstrated, Harvest-Lead-confirmed experience in the specific Experienced role they build on — for example, Ladder Picker requires prior Ladder Spotter experience. This is the one place where movement is gated by a specific prerequisite rather than general readiness. Remaining in Experienced roles long-term is equally valued.
Specialist Highest skill, judgment, or trust — requires demonstrated experience, not just time served. Not everyone needs to reach this tier — and that's fine

Includes Ladder Picker, Culler — In Situ, and Homeowner Interface. Each builds on a specific Experienced role or on broad cross-tier experience.

1

Ladder Picker

Specialist · Suggested order 1 of 3

Pick from height. Requires mastery of spotting fundamentals and physical comfort at elevation.

Prerequisites: Demonstrated, Harvest-Lead-confirmed experience as Ladder Spotter (Experienced). Must be comfortable at heights of 6–12 feet.

Responsibilities
  • Climb the ladder to reach fruit inaccessible from the ground or with the picking tool.
  • Pick fruit using correct technique (gentle grip, stem-twist) to avoid bruising.
  • Hand fruit down carefully to the Ground Gatherer, or place in a harvest bag worn over the shoulder.
  • Communicate clearly with the spotter before and during the climb.
  • Never overreach — descend and reposition the ladder rather than stretching to the side.
  • Announce intentions before moving: climbing, descending, or shifting weight.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Demonstrated experience as Ladder Spotter (Experienced tier).
  • Comfortable working at heights of 6–12 feet.
  • Physical fitness to climb and pick while maintaining balance.
  • Strong understanding of ladder safety, built through prior Ladder Spotter experience.
Where it can lead: Many volunteers remain in Ladder Picker long-term once qualified — a valid, skilled role in its own right. May be paired with other roles depending on harvest needs, at Harvest Lead discretion.
2

Culler — In Situ Quality Critical

Specialist · Suggested order 2 of 3

Make real-time quality decisions on-site, before produce is loaded. This role runs on every harvest — no exceptions.

Prerequisites: Comfortable across Foundational and Experienced roles. Must have reviewed the written culling criteria before first assignment.

Responsibilities
  • Perform on-site culling immediately before produce is loaded into the vehicle.
  • Assess each piece of fruit using the written culling criteria: harvest-quality, first-use (lightly damaged), pet-grade (moderately damaged / over-ripe), or compost (significantly damaged or molding).
  • Physically separate fruit into the correct streams.
  • Explain decisions to nearby volunteers when it creates a useful teaching moment.
  • Flag unusual quality issues or site-specific conditions to the Harvest Lead.
  • Ensure harvest-quality fruit is clean and properly crated before loading.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Comfortable across Foundational and Experienced roles.
  • Must have reviewed Iskashitaa's written culling criteria before first assignment.
  • Good judgment under time pressure, especially on Friday harvests when the crew is tired.
  • Accountability — culling decisions directly affect food safety for refugee families.
Where it can lead: Volunteers experienced as Culler are often well-suited for Homeowner Interface (Specialist, highest trust), at Harvest Lead or staff discretion. Remaining focused on culling — performed every harvest — is equally valid long-term.

On-site culling must occur on every harvest. If the designated Culler is absent, the Harvest Lead assigns this duty to the most experienced available volunteer. The culling criteria reference card must be present on-site at all times. This role was previously combined with Recorder — they are now separate so culling gets dedicated attention.

3

Homeowner Interface Highest Trust

Specialist · Suggested order 3 of 3

The face of Iskashitaa at every site. Build the relationship that brings homeowners back season after season.

Prerequisites: Demonstrated reliability and judgment across multiple harvests, typically including Experienced or Specialist roles. Appointed at Harvest Lead or staff discretion.

Responsibilities
  • Greet the homeowner warmly on arrival and thank them for their donation.
  • Briefly explain what the team will do and approximately how long it will take.
  • Answer basic questions about Iskashitaa's mission and where the fruit will go.
  • Deliver the donation envelope (financial ask) at close of harvest, per Iskashitaa protocol.
  • Leave the homeowner with a positive impression — this directly affects repeat harvest requests and financial contributions.
  • Report any concerns, special instructions, or access issues to the Harvest Lead.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Broad familiarity with the harvest process, typically gained through Experienced or Specialist roles, to answer homeowner questions confidently.
  • Strong interpersonal skills: warm, professional, a natural ambassador for Iskashitaa.
  • Comfort communicating in English; additional languages a significant asset.
  • Familiar with Iskashitaa's mission, programs, and current season impact data.
Where it can lead: This is the highest-trust role in the program. Appointment is at Harvest Lead or staff discretion — not automatic upon reaching the Specialist tier.

The homeowner relationship is the source of repeat harvests, financial donations, and referrals — treat every interaction as a long-term investment. Homeowners who express interest in deeper involvement can be connected to the Adopt-a-Harvest program.

Supporting Roles

Separate tracks, outside the tiers

Essential to every harvest, but filled by trained staff, designated experienced volunteers, or approved individuals meeting specific criteria — not reached by progressing through the three tiers.

HL

Harvest Lead

Supporting Role — Staff or Senior Volunteer

On-site manager for every harvest. Owns safety, role assignment, produce quality, and data integrity from briefing to debrief.

Prerequisites: Extensive experience across all three tiers (Foundational, Experienced, and Specialist). Completed Iskashitaa Harvest Lead training (criteria TBD). Appointed by staff.

Responsibilities
  • Know each volunteer's current tier and the roles they're comfortable with before the harvest begins.
  • Assign all roles to the crew before departure from the office.
  • Run the pre-harvest briefing: roles, site conditions, safety reminders.
  • Make all real-time safety decisions on-site.
  • Ensure on-site culling (Culler — In Situ) happens on every harvest — non-negotiable.
  • Collect completed harvest records from the Recorder and return them to the office.
  • Assess volunteer readiness to take on new roles or move between tiers and communicate recommendations to staff.
  • Serve as Homeowner Interface if that role is not yet filled.
  • Debrief the crew after each harvest; flag any issues to staff.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Extensive experience across all harvest roles.
  • Completed Iskashitaa Harvest Lead training (criteria to be defined by leadership).
  • Food safety awareness; familiarity with culling criteria and recording procedures.
  • Strong team management and interpersonal skills.
Where it can lead: N/A — this is a supporting role, not part of the tiered model. Harvest Leads are designated by staff.

The Harvest Lead is the single point of accountability for every on-site outcome. Training requirements, compensation/recognition, and formal designation criteria are pending leadership decisions — see Open Items.

DR

Driver

Supporting Role — Approved Volunteers

Transport the crew and produce safely between the Iskashitaa office and harvest sites.

Prerequisites: Valid driver's license. Clean driving record meeting Iskashitaa's insurance requirements. Completed Volunteer Driver orientation and background check. Approval separate from harvest tiers.

Responsibilities
  • Drive volunteers (including refugee participants) to and from harvest sites using Iskashitaa's approved vehicle.
  • Follow Iskashitaa's Volunteer Driver guidelines (iskashitaa.org/volunteer-driver).
  • Load and secure produce crates for transport — protect produce from shifting or bruising.
  • Maintain awareness of vehicle capacity and passenger safety at all times.
  • Coordinate departure times with the Harvest Lead.
  • Report any vehicle issues or incidents to staff immediately.
Skills & Qualities Needed
  • Valid driver's license; clean driving record per Iskashitaa insurance requirements.
  • Completed Volunteer Driver orientation and background check.
  • Comfort driving a cargo or passenger van with passengers.
  • Physical ability to assist with loading produce crates.
Where it can lead: N/A — Driver approval is a separate track, independent of tier or role.

Driver approval is independent of tier or role. An experienced Specialist-tier harvester is not automatically an approved driver. Driving refugee participants carries specific insurance and protocol requirements — confirm current requirements with staff before driving. Full protocols: iskashitaa.org/volunteer-driver.

MW

Mid-Week Storage Cull Volunteer

Supporting Role — Currently Vacant

A dedicated volunteer (ideally 1–2 people) who inspects stored produce mid-week — Tuesday or Wednesday — to remove deteriorating fruit before it spreads spoilage through the batch.

Responsibilities
  • Inspects all produce in storage against Iskashitaa's written culling criteria.
  • Makes independent culling decisions within a defined scope; flags issues outside that scope to staff.
  • Documents findings and any discards for staff review.
Why this role matters
  • Friday harvests sit in storage through the weekend — this role catches what deteriorates before Wednesday distribution.
  • Directly addresses the "no homeowner feedback" and inconsistent-culling gaps named in the Case for Change.

This role was filled consistently for six years by one volunteer. The practice worked — it simply needs to be re-established and given a recruiting push, not redesigned from scratch.
